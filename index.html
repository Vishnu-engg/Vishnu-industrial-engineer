Optimizing Disaster Relief Supply Chains

This project addresses the growing need for efficient humanitarian logistics in flood-prone regions of Kerala, India. By leveraging data-driven methodologies, the project develops an optimized disaster relief supply chain model designed to enhance emergency response and ensure equitable distribution of critical resources.

Key Contributions

  • Designed a tailored logistics network to handle the complex terrain and socio-economic challenges unique to Kerala.
  • Applied Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) and Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) to select optimal warehouse locations.
  • Integrated Monte Carlo Simulation to account for variability in demand for essential relief items during flood events.
  • Employed Python with PuLP (Linear Programming) to minimize transportation costs and delivery risks under different disaster scenarios.
  • Generated an adaptive and scalable supply chain model that supports the Kerala State Disaster Management Authority.

Tools & Technologies

Python NumPy pandas PuLP SimPy AHP/MCDA Monte Carlo Simulation Google Earth Excel Power BI

Impact

The final model enhances Kerala's disaster resilience by ensuring faster and more efficient delivery of humanitarian aid, reducing both costs and risks during flood emergencies. This approach also provides a scalable blueprint for other flood-prone regions globally.

Wastewater Treatment with Phytorid Technology

This project explores sustainable and eco-friendly methods for treating domestic wastewater using Phytorid Technology, a constructed wetland system. Conducted at the cafeteria of Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Technology (RIT), the study aims to demonstrate the potential of using natural elements for effective greywater treatment and reuse.

Key Contributions

  • Designed and evaluated a Phytorid-based greywater treatment system using a horizontal subsurface flow constructed wetland.
  • Implemented treatment using native plant species such as Canna Indica, Sunflower, and Colocasia Esculenta.
  • Analyzed treated water quality based on key physico-chemical parameters including pH, TDS, TSS, BOD, COD, and more.
  • Compared pre- and post-treatment results to assess contaminant reduction and validate the technology's effectiveness.

Tools & Techniques

On-site Sampling Laboratory Testing Statistical Analysis Wetland Construction Eco-design Principles Water Quality Assessment

Impact

This project demonstrates a low-cost, low-maintenance, and environmentally sustainable solution for greywater management in institutional settings. It highlights the potential of Phytorid Technology as a replicable model for urban wastewater treatment and promotes the reuse of treated water for non-potable applications, thereby conserving freshwater resources.
